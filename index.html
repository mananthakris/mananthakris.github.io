01 About The shape of the work

The work I care about sits at the seam where architecture, organizations, and product reality meet.

Based · Austin, Texas
Focus · Applied AI & Platform
Domains · PropTech, Telecom, EdTech
Background · 20 years in engineering

I've spent two decades building distributed systems and the engineering organizations that ship them, across enterprise, growth-stage, and startup environments. The arc of my work has moved from scaling infrastructure, to scaling teams, to scaling intelligence into the systems we already operate.

Lately that means applied AI in the wild: agentic systems with real evals, RAG pipelines that hold up under production load, and MLOps work that closes the loop between training and monitoring. I'm especially interested in the parts that don't show up in demos. Boundary design between agents. Evaluation as an engineering discipline. The org structures that make AI delivery durable rather than performative.

This site is where I keep that work in the open. Some of it is finished. Most of it is in motion. That's the point.

04 Themes Ideas I keep returning to

The questions worth chasing.

Three threads run through most of what I build and write. They're less "areas of expertise" and more "things I keep wanting to understand better."

AI as engineering, not demos

The gap between an LLM prototype and a system that holds up in production is mostly engineering: evaluation harnesses, retrieval quality, agent boundaries, observability. Most of my project work is about closing that gap and learning what production-grade actually looks like for AI.

Architecture meets organization

System design and org design are the same problem viewed from different angles. The boundaries you draw between services tend to mirror the boundaries you draw between teams, and the inverse. I'm interested in how to design both well, especially as AI changes what either should look like.

Pragmatism over orthodoxy

I don't believe in pre-optimizing for scale, and I don't believe in retrofitting trends into systems that don't need them. The interesting question is always: what's the right level of durability for the phase we're in? That tension shows up in every project here.
