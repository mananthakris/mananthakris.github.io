I've spent two decades building distributed systems and the engineering organizations that ship them, across enterprise, growth-stage, and startup environments. The arc of my work has moved from scaling infrastructure, to scaling teams, to scaling intelligence into the systems we already operate.

04 Themes Ideas I keep returning to

The questions worth chasing.

Three threads run through most of what I build and write. Less "areas of expertise" and more "things I keep wanting to understand better."

AI as engineering, not demos

The gap between an LLM prototype and a system that holds up in production is mostly engineering: evaluation harnesses, retrieval quality, agent boundaries, observability. Most of my project work is about closing that gap.

Architecture meets organization

System design and org design are the same problem viewed from different angles. The boundaries you draw between services tend to mirror the boundaries you draw between teams. I'm interested in how to design both well.

Pragmatism over orthodoxy

I don't believe in pre-optimizing for scale, and I don't believe in retrofitting trends into systems that don't need them. The interesting question is always: what's the right level of durability for the phase we're in?
